Terms, Headings and Subheadings of Hypertension Guideline Display  

Absolute Risk Reduction (ARR)* is the difference in the event rate between control group (CER) and treated group (EER): ARR = CER - EER.
Adverse events- any allergy or adverse reaction data recorded in the software "CPRS: Adverse Reaction Tracking (ART)" in VISTA for the patient. Indicated by a black circle in the "Reasons" column in the recommendations tab, for drug specific reactions.
"Assumption: BP not under control" tab- When there is no BP in the computer database for a patient, the program calculates the recommendations under two different assumptions, i.e. BP under control and BP not under control. This tab displays recommendations assuming the patients' BP has not met his or her individual target level.
"Assumption: BP under control" tab- When there is no BP in the computer database for a patient, the program calculates the recommendations under two different assumptions, i.e. BP under control and BP not under control. This tab displays recommendations assuming the patients' BP has met his or her individual target level.
"Assumptions" tab- displays assumptions being made by ATHENA when generating recommendations.
ATHENA DSS- A computerized decision support system for the management of hypertension based on the report by The Sixth Report of the Joint National Committee on prevention, Detection, Evaluation, and Treatment of High Blood Pressure, the VHA/DOD Clinical Practice guideline for Diagnosis and Management of Hypertension in the Primary Care Setting and updated with new evidence as deemed appropriate.
BP- blood pressure
"BP-Prescription Graphs" tab - displays blood pressure historical data and prescription historical data in easy to read charts.
"Click here for important..." heading - Important information for individual drug recommendations. Clicking on the "More Info" button will show relevant information related to specific drug recommendation.
"Comments" tab- displays patient's individual risk group and general messages related to hypertension management.
Compelling indication- A JNC 6 term defined as "...compelling indications for specific agents in certain clinical conditions, based on outcomes data from randomized controlled trials" (page 35). Indicated by an icon (blue checkmark in green circle) in the "Reasons" column in the recommendations tab.
"Consider the following options" heading- ATHENA lists recommendations without prioritizing them. The primary care physician can evaluate individual recommendations and decide which one(s) is (are) most appropriate for the individual patient.
Contraindication- Any condition, especially any condition of disease, which renders some particular line of treatment improper or undesirable (On-line Medical Dictionary ). Indicated by an icon (red circle with white X) in the "Reasons" column in the recommendations tab.
CPRS (Computerized Patient Record System)- A VISTA software application to view electronic medical records used in the VA Health Care System.
"Feedback" heading- This checkbox gives the clinician the opportunity to disagree with the drug recommendations generated by ATHENA. A comments box at the bottom of the page can be used to detail disagreements or suggestions.
"Glossary" tab- displays definitions of headings, subheadings and terms used in the output display of the Hypertension Guideline.
Guideline Goal- the goal for BP control displayed on the screen in the pop-up window. This is the patient's target BP for adequate control of BP. Target BP is set as per JNC 6, based on co morbidities.  ATHENA uses co morbidities included in the patients' purpose of visit (VPOV) file in CPRS.
Hypertension Advisory- The header that appears at the top of the ATHENA DSS interactive window in CPRS. 
"Info" button - displays relevant information related to a specific drug recommendation. See also "Click here for more important..."
Isolated Systolic Hypertension- is defined as pre-treatement SBP>140mmHg and DBP<90mmHg (JNC 6 table 2 pg 11). ATHENA uses this definition for treated patients as well. No adjustments are made for patients with diabetes, heart failure or renal insufficiency, whose target BP is lower than 130/85mmHg.
"Medication adherence" tab - displays recommendations to improve patients' medication adherence.
Most recent BP in database- this is the most recent blood pressure recorded in the "Vitals / Measurements" application of VISTA or the BP entered by the clinician into "Today's Decision BP" whichever is most recent. The corresponding date is displayed in the "Date" field.
"Non-pharmacologic management'' tab- displays individual recommendations for lifestyle modifications (diet, exercise, smoking cessation, alcohol consumption) to improve the management of hypertension.
Number Needed to Treat (NNT) is the number of patients who need to be treated to prevent one bad outcome. It is the inverse of the ARR: 1/ARR
Odds Ratio*- describes the odds of an experimental patient suffering an adverse event relative to a control patient.
Prescription expiration- If a prescription expires, it will not appear on the active medication list though the patient may still be taking the medication. This will be reflected correctly in the Prescription graph.
"Reasons" heading- The reason for a drug recommendation based on the patient's co morbidities. Reasons are displayed with the appropriate icon indicating if there is a compelling indication, relative indication, contraindication, relative contraindication or adverse events related to that drug recommendation based on the patients' co morbidities registered in CPRS.
"Recommendations" tab- this tab displays drug recommendations for the management of hypertension. The recommendations tab is displayed when the pop-up window appears.
Relative contraindication- as in JNC VI  "May have unfavorable effects on comorbid conditions". Indicated by a yellow circle in the "Reasons" column in the recommendations tab.
Relative indication- as in JNC VI  "May have favorable effects on comorbid conditions". Indicated by a blue circle in the "Reasons" column in the recommendations tab.
Relative Risk - is the ratio of risk in the treated group (EER) to the risk in the control group (CER): RR = EER/CER. RR is used in randomised trials and cohort studies.
Relative Risk Reduction (RRR)*- is the percent reduction in events in the treated group event rate (EER) compared to the control group event rate (CER): RRR=(CER-EER)/CER*100
Risk Group A - defined as patients with high-normal blood pressure or stage 1, 2 or 3 hypertension who do not have clinical cardiovascular disease, target organ damage, or other risk factors (JNC 6).
Risk Group B- defined as patients with hypertension who do not have clinical cardiovascular disease or target organ damage but have one or more of the following risk factors: smoking, dyslipidemia, age older than 60 years, men and postmenopausal women and/or family history of cardiovascular disease (women under age 65 and men under age 55) (JNC6).
Risk Gropup C- defined as patients with hypertension who have clinically manifest diabetes and/or cardiovascular disease or target organ damage (left ventricular hypertrophy, angina/prior myocardial infarction, prior coronary revascularization, herat failure, stroke, transient ischemic attack, nephropathy, peripheral arterial disease and/or retinopathy.
Today's decision BP- this field may be filled in with the BP on which the clinician will base today's management decision. It may be the BP observed at the visit or a synthesis of several BP's including home BP measurements. The BP inserted in this field will not be registered in the VISTA package; it will be stored in the ATHENA database and is used as the most recent BP until superseded by another. The corresponding date is the date for the current clinic appointment. Please press "Update advisory" button after entering BP in this field so recommendations can be updated.
"Update advisory" button- displays updated recommendations based on the BP inserted in the Today's decision BP field.
VISTA- "Veterans Health Information Systems and Technology Architecture" is the VA computer system.
* definitions extracted from the Evidence-Based Medicine Glossary in the Centre for Evidence Based Medicine Webpage (http://cebm.jr2.ox.ac.uk/docs/glossary.html)
