Last Updated: November, 2001

  • In high risk ( 1 cardiovascular risk factor) hypertensive patients, doxazosin (alpha blocker) compared with chlorthalidone (diuretic) was associated with a 25% increased risk of cardiovascular events and 19% increased risk of stroke in 3.3 years (ALLHAT 2000). The doxazosin arm of this trial was discontinued due to these increased risks.
  • Mean SBP was 2-3mmHg higher in the doxazosin group as compared to the chlorthalidone group. Mean DBP was the same.
  • Doxazosin had a lower adherence rate (75%) than chlorthalidone (86%).
