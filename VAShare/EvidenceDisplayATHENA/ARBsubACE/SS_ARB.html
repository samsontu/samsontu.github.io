Because they are not associated with the intractable dry cough seen in some patients taking ACE inhibitors, angiotensin receptor blockers are indicated in patients who require an ACE inhibitor but who cannot tolerate it due to drug-induced dry cough.(Therapeutics Letter, issue 28, January/February/March 1999)

Angiotensin-II Receptor Antagonists: Their Place in Therapy
JULIENNE K. KIRK, PHARM.D.,
Wake Forest University School of Medicine, Winston-Salem, North Carolina

Angiotensin-II receptor antagonists are well tolerated. Cough occurs much less often with these agents than with angiotensin-converting enzyme inhibitors, and they do not adversely affect lipid profiles or cause rebound hypertension after discontinuation. Clinical trials indicate that angiotensin-II receptor antagonists are effective and safe in the treatment of hypertension.
