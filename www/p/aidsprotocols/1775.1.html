INCLUSION CRITERIA (from Section 5.0)

5.1 Inclusion Criteria

5.11 Written documentation of positive antibody to HIV by any federally licensed ELISA test kit. This test should be confirmed by another method, for example, Western blot, RIA, HIV culture, etc. If a prior diagnosis of AIDS has been established by CDC criteria, a confirmatory test is not required.

5.12 Male or non-pregnant (evidence by a negative pregnancy test results within 30 days of randomization).

5.13 Age: > or = to 13 years old.

5.14 Karnofsky > or = to 60, or in the clinician's opinion that the patient has a reasonable chance of surviving to end of study.

5.15 Positive PPD skin test (defined as > or = to 5 mm in duration to 5-TU of PPD using Mantoux method, to be read between 48 and 72 hours after application) but without evidence of active clinical tuberculosis.

OR

Documented history of a positive PPD skin test WITHOUT subsequent treatment or prophylaxis with more than one month of any antimycobacterial medication.

NOTE: While the optimal time for reading the PPD is between 48 and 72 hours, positive reactions beyond 72 hours are acceptable.

5.16 Laboratory

Hgb > 8 gm/dl

ANC > 750 cells/ul

Platelets > 50,000/ul

Total bilirubin < or = to 2.5 mg/dl

AST or SGOT < or = to 5.0 X normal

Alkaline phosphates < or = to 5.0 X normal
