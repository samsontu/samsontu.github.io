10.0 Sample Size Calculationss

The sample size calculations are based on the following estimates of clinical and microbiologic treatment failure: we predict that patients treated with a 7-day course of fluconazole 100 mg daily will have an incidence of clinical treatment failure of <= 5%, and an incidence of clinical relapse of 5% at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy. Patients who receive a single dose of 200 mg of fluconazole will have an incidence of clinical treatment failure of 15%, and an incidence of clinical relapse at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy of 30%. At a power of 0.80 and an alpha of 0.05, a minimum of 356 evaluable patients would be required to demonstrate a statistical difference in clinical treatment failure, and a minimum of 100 evaluable patients would be required to demonstrate a statistical difference in clinical relapse at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy.

However, we predict that patients treated with a 7-day course of fluconazole 100 mg daily will have an incidence of microbiological treatment failure of <= 5%, and an incidence of microbiological relapse of 10% at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy. But patients who receive a single dose of 200 mg of fluconazole will have an incidence of microbiological treatment failure of 25%, and an incidence of microbiological relapse at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy of 45%. At a power of 0.80 and an alpha of 0.05, a minimum of 70 evaluable treatment episodes are required to demonstrate a statistical difference in microbiological relapse at 26-32 days after initiation of therapy.

Furthermore, we predict that there will be a maximal incidence of clinical relapse or recurrent infection of 60% at 3 months after initiation of treatment for each study arm, regardless of the treatment received. However, the time to clinical relapse or recurrent infection may be different between the treatment arms.
