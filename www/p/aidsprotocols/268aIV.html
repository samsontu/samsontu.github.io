APPENDIX IV

ACTG OPPOTUNISTIC INFECTION COMMITTEE

ENDPOINT CRITERIA FOR PNEUMOCYSTIS CARINII PNEUMONIA

CONFIRMED PNEUMOCYSTIS CARINII PNEUMONIA:

1. Either:

a) a compatible clinical syndrome as described below for probable infection,

b) an abnormal chest radiograph, (or CT scan).

-AND-

2. Histological or cytological evidence of Pneumocystis carinii on bronchoalveolar lavage, lung biopsy or sputum specimen

PROBABLE PNEUMOCYSTIS CARINII PNEUMONIA:

1. A history (within the past three months) of shortness of breath, dyspnea on exertion, cough or fever

-AND-

2. Abnormal chest x-ray (or CT scan) or hypoxemic arterial blood gas, P(alpha)O(2) < 80 mm Hg or (A-a)DO(2) mm Hg > 15.

-AND-

3. Patient was treated with specific anti-pneumocystis therapy.

CONFIRMED EXTRAPULMONARY PNEUMOCYSTOSIS:

1. Histological, cytological or autopsy evidence of extrapulmonary pneumocystosis.

PROBABLE EXTRAPULMONARY PNEUMOCYSTOSIS (eye disease only):

1. Pneumocystis lesions of the retina as indicated by characteristic lesions consistent with Pneumocystis choroiditis according to an experienced opthalmologist.

AND

2. Clinical improvement with systemic therapy.
