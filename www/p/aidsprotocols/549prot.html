Protocol Synopsis

Title:

CCTG 549 Evaluation of Clarithromycin And Clofazimine With And Without Ethambutol In The Therapy Of Disseminated Mycobacterium avium Complex Infection In Patients With AIDS

Objectives:

Primary:

1. Compare the combination of clarithromycin and clofazimine with and without ethambutol to sustain clinical and microbiologic remission of disseminated MAC in AIDS patients.

2. Compare the rate at which MAC isolates become resistant in the treatment arms.

Secondary:

1. Compare toxicity in the treatment arms.

2. Compare survival in the treatment arms.

Study Design:

Phase II open-label randomized study

Arm A: Clarithromycin 1,000 mg bid

Clofazimine 100 mg/day

Ethambutol 400 mg bid

Arm B: Clarithromycin 1,000 mg bid

Clofazimine 100 mg/day

Patients:

60-80 evaluable cases of disseminated MAC in AIDS patients.

Randomization:

Central randomization with stratification by site and empiric therapy.

Total Study Duration:

12 months after last patient is accrued.

Evaluation:

Primary Endpoints

1. Recrudescence of microbiologic MAC disease.

2. Emergence of MAC resistance to study drugs.

Secondary Endpoints:

1. Proportion with dose limiting toxicity.

2. Mortality (proportion and time to event).

3. Time to sterile blood cultures.

4. Time to resolution of clinical symptoms.
