4.6 PHARMACY

4.61 Drug Supply, Distribution and Storage

The study medications will be available through the Clinical Research Products Repository. The ACTU pharmacist can obtain study drugs from the Clinical Research Products Repository by following the ordering procedures given in the manual Pharmacy Guidelines and Instructions for AIDS Clinical Trials Group in the section Investigational Drug Control.

NVP and matching placebo will be provided by Boehringer Ingelheim Pharmaceuticals, Inc., ZDV will be provided by Burroughs-Wellcome Co., and ddI and matching placebo will be supplied by Bristol-Myers Squibb Company. All study drugs should be stored at room temperature, between 15 and 30 C (59 and 86 F).

4.62 Drug Accountability

The ACTU pharmacist is required to maintain complete records of all study medications received from the NIAID Clinical Research Products Repository and subsequently dispensed. All unused zidovudine, didanosine and nevirapine must be returned to the NIAID Clinical Research Products Repository after the study is completed or terminated. All unused or partially used NVP must be returned to the NIAID Clinical Research Products Repository. The procedures to be followed are given in the manual Pharmacy Guidelines and Instructions for AIDS Clinical Trials Group in the sections on Investigational Drug Control and Drug Dispensing.
