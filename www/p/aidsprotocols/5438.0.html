8.0 Dose Modification/Toxicity Management

8.1 Dose modification/Toxicity

Adverse events will be graded according to the "NIAID Recommendation for Grading of Acute and Subacute Adverse Events" (Appendix D). All grade 4 toxicities will be reported to Roerig Division, Pfizer Inc. Only other unexpected toxicity which the investigator attributes to the study medications should be reported to Pfizer, Inc.

8.2 Gastrointestinal Toxicity

Gastrointestinal toxicity (nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea) should be managed with split dosing, anti-emetics, or anti-motility agents. All efforts should be made to exclude other causes of these symptoms. If the study investigators attribute gastrointestinal toxicity to study medications, all medications should be stopped. After discussion with the principal investigator, the patient may be rechallenged with fluconazole.

8.3 Laboratory toxicity

Liver function test abnormalities

Moderate and marked liver abnormalities will be defined as follows:


		ALT/AST			Alkaline		Total

					Phosphatase		Bilirubin



Moderate	>5-10 X ULN		>5-10 X ULN		>3-5 X ULN

Marked		>10 X ULN		>10 X ULN		>5 X ULN

where ULN is defined as the upper limit of normal if the pretreatment baseline was normal, or the pretreatment baseline if it was abnormal.

Moderately abnormal liver function tests should be repeated within 3 to 7 days. If they are confirmed they should be repeated at weekly intervals until they resolve.

Patients with marked liver function abnormalities, i.e. > 10 X ULN for AST, ALT, or alkaline phosphatase or >5 X ULN for bilirubin, must discontinue treatment immediately. The abnormal tests should be repeated within 48 hours, and then at 3 to 7 day intervals until they resolve. Additional evaluation of patients with marked abnormalities that persist after study drug treatment is discontinued should be considered. The additional evaluation may include gastrointestinal consultation and additional laboratory tests (e.g., HBV, HAV, HCV, CMV serology or MAC blood cultures).

Rechallenge may be considered only after the responsible IRB, the site co-investigator, the principal investigator and the patient agree.
