PREFACE

The Terry Beirn Community Programs for Clinical Research on AIDS (CPCRA) is funded by the National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases (NIAID) of the National Institutes of Health (NIH). The CPCRA was created to extend the opportunity for participation in AIDS research activities to community-based clinicians and their patients through the establishment of treatment research programs in communities where HIV-infected patients live and receive their primary medical care. A major goal of the CPCRA is to access HIV-infected populations frequently underrepresented in clinical trials. Hence, studies conducted by the CPCRA are directed toward all persons with HIV infection but are specifically targeted for African Americans, Latinos/Hispanics, women, and injection drug users (IDUs).

For purposes of standardization, a framework is in place which guides and supports the research effort. Specific instructions are provided to investigators and research staff in the CPCRA Investigator's Handbook, Data Collection Handbook (1), the Clinical Events Handbook, and the Pharmacy Guidelines and Instructions for Community Programs for Clinical Research on AIDS, which are the crux of this framework. CPCRA policies and procedures common to all research protocols are given in the CPCRA Investigator's Handbook and Data Collection Handbook.

In addition to the CPCRA references cited above, each CPCRA protocol has a manual of operations. The manual of operations accompanies the protocol and provides specific direction for proper implementation of the study, completion of the case report forms, and other protocol-specific issues.

A comprehensive education and training plan has also been developed for each CPCRA protocol. Each plan includes a full spectrum of education and training materials designed to ensure that CPCRA investigators have a broad-based knowledge of the disease process as well as the technical skills needed to conduct the protocol in a scientifically sound manner. All CPCRA clinical staff must complete the training requirements prior to enrolling any patients in the protocol.

The purpose of the PCP-INT2 prophylaxis protocol is to compare the safety and efficacy of two therapies for the prevention of primary or secondary Pneumocystis carinii pneumonia (PCP) in high-risk HIV-infected patients who are intolerant of trimethoprim and/or sulfonamides. The protocol document provides the background and rationale, the study design, the eligibility requirements, the sample size and the assumptions used to arrive at the sample size, the methodology for implementation of the study, and the plan for analysis of the data. This protocol is being carried out with the support of the NIAID, Burroughs Wellcome Company, and Jacobus Pharmaceutical Company, Inc.

(1) Effective August 1994, all standard procedures for completing case report forms in the CPCRA Investigator's Handbook will be superseded by procedures outlined in a new CPCRA Data Collection Handbook.
