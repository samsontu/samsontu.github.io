2.0 STUDY OBJECTIVES

2.1 The Primary Objectives of the study are to investigate:

2.11 Activity as measured by CD4+ lymphocyte response, and the reduction in HIV viral load as measured by quantitative peripheral blood mononuclear cell virus cultures.

2.12 The safety and tolerance of the 3 different treatment arms.

2.2 The Secondary Objectives of the study are to investigate:

2.21 The pharmacokinetic parameters (Cmax, tmax, AUCs, urinary excretion) for the three drugs administered in different combinations.

2.22 Exploratory population pharmacometrics.

2.23 The reduction in HIV viral burden as measured by using plasma viremia, p24 antigen, and the RNA/DNA polymerase chain reaction (PCR).

2.24 The development of HIV resistance to Ro 31-8959, ddC and ZDV

2.25 Changes in clinical status, as measured by: weight, Karnofsky Score, disease progression, and development of Opportunistic Infections.

2.26 Other immunological tests: CD8, neopterin, Beta 2 microglobulin, CD4%, CD4/CD38/HLA-DR and CD8/CD38/HLA-DR activation markers.

2.27 The effect that the different treatment arms have on the patient's quality of life using a standardized instrument.
