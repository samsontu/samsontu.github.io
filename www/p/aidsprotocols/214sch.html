SCHEMA

TITLE:

A Phase I Comparative Blinded Trial of Several HIV-1 Derived Immunogens in Infected Individuals with >= 500 CD4 Cells/mm^3

GOAL:

To compare several HIV-1 derived immunogens in HIV-infected individuals with CD4 >= 500, and to determine whether there are significant advantages to any one of these vaccines.

DESIGN:

Randomized Phase I Trial

Participants will be randomized to receive one of the following products:

RANDOMIZATION

o IIIB based, HIV-1 gp 120 prod. in CHO cells in alum adjuvant N = 26

(Genentech) - monthly injections

o MW based, HIV-1 gp 120 prod. in CHO cells in alum adjuvant N = 26

(Genentech) - monthly injections

o SF-2 based, HIV-1 gp 120/MF-59 (CHO cells) N = 26

(Chiron) - monthly injections

o SF-2 based, HIV-1 Env - 2-3/MF-59/MTP-PE (yeast) N=26

(Chiron) - monthly injections

o Alum or MF-59/MTP-PE (10 micro g)/vehicle N = 26

monthly injections

REGIMEN:

Subjects will be vaccinated over a 36 week period of time (weeks 0, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 28, and 36). If significant immunologic changes are induced among vaccine recipients in the absence of demonstrable deleterious effects, individuals who initially received control immunogen may, after discussion with the sponsors and FDA, be offered vaccination with one of the immunogens which has elicited novel immune response in a subsequent protocol amendment.

POPULATION:

n = 130 (26 patients in each of the 4 vaccine arms and 13 patients in each of the 2 control arms (Alum or MF-59/MTP-PE).

ENDPOINTS:

1 o An increase in HIV-1 specific neutralization titers, HIV-1 gp120 specific ELISA, or HIV-1 specific CTL or proliferative responses.

o HIV-1 specific CTL responses.

o The effect of neutralization antibodies to MN and autologous isolates.

2 o Change in the slope of CD4 counts over time.

o The development of high grade toxicities or intolerance.
