4.1 STUDY ENDPOINTS

4.11 Primary Endpoint

4.111 The primary endpoint of this study will be the time to development of confirmed active tuberculosis. This includes any confirmed active pulmonary or extra-pulmonary Mycobacterium tuberculosis disease.

4.112 A more inclusive analyses using probable clinical tuberculosis in combination with confirmed clinical tuberculosis and death will be performed to support the conclusions of the primary analysis.

Confirmed clinical tuberculosis requires a positive culture from any source, i.e. sputum, blood, urine, stool, etc. for Mycobacterium tuberculosis.

Probable clinical tuberculosis requires:

Objective evidence of clinical tuberculosis based on history and/or physical examination PLUS radiograph, CAT scan, and/or other diagnostic tests, but without other concurrent illness which would explain the findings.

PLUS EITHER

Response to antituberculous therapy, i.e., resolution or improvement of clinical signs and symptoms, AND objective improvement of radiograph, and other diagnostic tests used in the presumptive diagnosis of tuberculosis

OR

Evidence of granuloma with organisms positive for AFB at autopsy. Note: Culture and speciation of autopsy material for Mtb should be attempted.

Positive acid-fast bacilli (AFB) smears from sputum, blood, urine, stool or other body fluids (except as stated above) are inadequate for the diagnosis of confirmed or probable clinical tuberculosis.

4.12 Secondary Endpoint

The combined endpoints of death and confirmed or probable clinical tuberculosis, toxicity from treatment, and laboratory findings will be used as secondary endpoints to address some of the secondary objectives of the study.
