3.0 STUDY DESIGN

This is a prospective, double-blind randomized controlled trial comparing fluconazole 200 mg orally once weekly versus fluconazole 50 mg once daily as prophylactic therapy of vulvovaginal candidiasis in a group of HIV infected women at high risk for Candida vaginitis. Patients will be randomized to receive either regimen for a period of six months. Patients who have a history of > or = to 2 episodes of Candidia vaginitis within 6 months before study entry (at least one of which is documented by a health care worker), or who have current acute Candida vaginal infection may be eligible for the study. Patients who have untreated acute infection who have not been treated will be uniformly treated with oral fluconazole 100 mg once daily for seven days prior to entry, and then begin study medication. Those that have begun a treatment regimen for an acute episode of Candidia vaginitis, documented by a positive KOH smear or fungal culture, within the last 10 days should complete their designated course of therapy prior to initiation of study medications.

Fungal cultures for Candida spp. will be obtained prior to therapy and at the time of recurrent infection from both vaginal and rectal sites. A sample of Candida isolates from those patients with recurrent infection will be speciated and their antifungal susceptibilities examined. DNA typing of isolates as described by Scherer and Stevens (18) will be performed in order to define the mechanism of recurrent infection.

In addition, the incidence of other fungal infections, including oral mucocutaneous candidiasis, esophageal candidiasis, as well as deep fungal infections, will be assessed in this trial. Resistance of fungal organisms to fluconazole will be determined.

The duration of the study is 6 months from enrollment, and enrollment will proceed over a two-year period (with the option of increasing the duration depending on the rate of accrual).
