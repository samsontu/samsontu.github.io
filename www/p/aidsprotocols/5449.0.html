9.0 Study Endpoints

9.1 Recurrent Candidia vaginitis

The primary efficacy variables will be:

9.11 The occurrence of recurrent Candidia vaginitis (defined as recurrent vaginal signs or symptoms, and microbiologic evidence of candidiasis based on a positive fungal culture on Sabouraud medium).

9.12 The time to recurrent vaginitis (clinical treatment failure).

9.13 The occurrence of non-albicans Candidia spp.

9.14 The occurrence of newly identified resistance in the isolate causing clinical relapse or recurrent infection.

9.2 Deep fungal disease

9.21 Cryptococcal Disease

All patients who develop fever without an obvious source should have a serum cryptococcal antigen titer obtained. All patients with a positive serum cryptococcal antigen should have a lumbar puncture, chest radiograph and urine fungal culture to define the extent of their disease. If no source of cryptococcus is found, the serum antigen test should be repeated and the patient closely followed.

Cryptococcal disease is defined as:

9.211 A positive cryptococcal culture or India ink from blood, CSF, urine or other sterile site.

9.212 A positive sputum culture for cryptococcus in the setting of pulmonary infiltrates. Time to event will be at the first visit at which pneumonia was identified.

9.213 A positive India ink from CSF.

9.22 Candida

9.221 A positive blood culture or culture from an otherwise sterile site (e.g. peritoneal or pleural fluid).

9.23 Endemic mycosis (coccidioidomycosis, histoplasmosis, blastomycosis) 9.231 A positive culture from an infected site or a positive biopsy.
