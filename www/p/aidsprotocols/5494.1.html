4.1 Inclusion Criteria

1. HIV positive and 13 years or older.

2. Patient should either;

a) have clinical symptoms consistent with disseminated MAC infection and at least one positive blood culture growing AFB/MAC within the previous 90 days.

b) be asymptomatic with two blood cultures positive for AFB/MAC that were obtained within two months prior to entry into the trial.

c) have clinical signs and symptoms consistent with disseminated MAC which must include fever > 7 days plus at least one of the following: night sweats, weight loss, diarrhea, hepatomegaly, splenomegaly, elevation of alkaline phosphatase, or severe anemia [Hgb < 8.5], but blood cultures have not been collected during this illness or have been incubating for < 3 weeks.*

d) have a positive tissue biopsy (eg. gut, liver, skin, bone marrow) with AFB/MAC but blood cultures have not been collected or have been incubating for < 3 weeks.**

3. If of child-bearing potential, the patient must have a licensed pregnancy test performed with negative result, and be utilizing effective contraceptive methods.

4. Patient meets the following laboratory parameters within two weeks prior to study start date.

a) Total neutrophil count >500/mm^3.

b) Creatinine < 2.5 x the upper limit of normal or Creatinine clearance > 30 ml/min.

c) SGOT (AST) and SGPT (ALT) < 10 x the upper limit of normal.

5. Patient has given written informed consent to participate in the trial.

* Patients whose prestudy blood cultures remain negative for MAC will be removed from study therapy and considered ineligible for evaluation.

** Patients with positive tissue biopsies but negative blood cultures for MAC will remain on study and be evaluated for primary endpoints, toxicity, and survival.
