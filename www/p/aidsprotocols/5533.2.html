3.2 Exclusion Criteria

1. Evidence of cryptococcal meningitis (+ CSF antigen, + CSF India Ink, + CSF Gram stain, + CSF culture); patients whose CSF India ink and cryptococcal antigen are negative but who eventually are found to have a positive culture will be excluded.

2. Allergy to imidazole, azole, flucytosine

3. ANC < 500. Patients with ANC between 500 and 1,000 can receive flucytosine at 1/2 the standard dose (see section 7.24). Patients should receive g-CSF to support the WBC and allow for full dosing of flucytosine.

4. Platelets < 50,000/mm^3. Patients with platelets < 50,000/mm^3 may enter the trial at the discretion of the investigator. Flucytosine should be dose reduced (if used at all) in this group of patients.

5. Abnormal hepatic chemistries. Patients with elevated AST, ALT, bilirubin and alkaline phosphatase may enter the trial at the discretion of the investigator. Patients with marked abnormalities (AST, ALT, Alkaline phosphatase > 5x ULN [upper limit of normal] or bilirubin > 2.5 x ULN) should be followed carefully for exacerbation of these abnormalities.

6. Prior systemic antifungal therapy (amphotericin B >= 1 mg/kg, fluconazole > 200 mg/d, ketoconazole > 200 mg/day, or itraconazole at 200 mg/day given within the last 30 days).

7. Pregnancy or lactation

8. Prior treatment for cryptococcal meningitis or extraneural cryptococcosis, histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis, blastomycosis, disseminated candida infection or other systemic mycosis.

9. Patients under evaluation or treatment for other acute opportunistic infections. (Patients may be receiving maintenance therapy for CMV retinitis or MAI)
